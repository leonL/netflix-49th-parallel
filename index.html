Netflix' 49th Parallel

In addition to more new releases, the U.S. site also has a deeper back catalogue than the Canadian version.

As might be expected, Netflix in the U.S. has significantly more recent films (if not necessarily better ones) than in Canada, but how do the two different back catalogues compare?

It turns out the one in the U.S. has more past titles by almost the same magnitude than it has more recent ones, and that's when you draw the line between past and recent at 15 years ago, quite far back in terms of the common shelf life of a movie. Categorizing Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon (2000) for example, as a recent release seems questionable at best, but for our present purpose the somewhat loose definition only underlines the point.

Not suprisingly, Netflix' concentration is on recent releases, and the distribution of title counts by release year for both catalogues bear out as much. Netflix bulks up on films made within the last two to three years, while paring down on titles as they age. Which of today's releases will continue to be available decade after decade is anyone's guess, if we still even have Netflix a decade from now.
